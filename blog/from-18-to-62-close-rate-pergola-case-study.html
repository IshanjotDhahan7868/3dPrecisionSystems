From 18% to 62% Close Rate: One Pergola Company's Story

A detailed look at how a mid-size outdoor living company tripled their close rate in 60 days with a single product configurator.

ID
Ishanjot Dhahan
Apr 17, 2025 · 9 min read · Precision 3D Technologies

In the spring of 2024, we got a call from a pergola and deck company based in the mid-Atlantic. They had been in business for 11 years, had a solid reputation, and were generating a healthy volume of inbound leads through Google and referrals. Their problem wasn't leads. It was closes.

"We get 40 to 50 inquiries a month," their sales director told us. "We close maybe 8. I know we're losing people somewhere in the process but I can't figure out where."

The Company

Evergreen Outdoor Living is a mid-size outdoor structure company with 14 full-time employees, a fabrication shop, and a four-person sales team. They sell pergolas, decks, covered patios, and outdoor kitchens — average ticket around $12,000.

Before the configurator, their website was a standard brochure site: photos of previous projects, a list of services, and a "get a free quote" form that routed to their sales inbox.

Before the Configurator

The typical sales process looked like this:

  1. Lead submits form or calls in
  2. Rep follows up within 24–48 hours (sometimes longer)
  3. Discovery call: 30–45 minutes to understand project scope, budget, timeline
  4. Site visit or Zoom to see the space
  5. Rep manually builds a quote in spreadsheet — takes 2–3 days
  6. Quote sent via email
  7. Follow-up calls over next 1–2 weeks
  8. Most prospects either went dark or chose a competitor

Close rate: 18%. Average time to close: 3 weeks. Cost per closed deal in rep time alone: over $800.

18%
Starting close rate — closing fewer than 1 in 5 qualified inbound leads despite solid reputation and healthy lead volume.

The Decision to Build

After our initial analysis, it was clear that the drop-off happened in two places: the gap between inquiry and first contact (leads going cold while waiting for a callback), and the gap between first call and formal quote (leads going to competitors who quoted faster).

The solution was a pergola configurator that would give prospects an instant ballpark price and let them design their structure before ever talking to a rep. The configurator would do the work of the first discovery call — and send pre-qualified, pre-configured leads directly to the sales team.

What We Built

We built a 3D pergola configurator with the following functionality:

  • Drag-to-resize width and depth with live price updates
  • Material selector: cedar, redwood, aluminum, and vinyl with visual swatches
  • Roof style selector: open slat, louvered, solid polycarbonate
  • Add-on toggles: integrated lighting, ceiling fan mount, privacy screens, outdoor heater rough-in
  • Optional attachment type: freestanding vs. house-attached (with price differential)
  • Instant quote summary with itemized breakdown
  • Lead capture form that sends the full configuration to the CRM along with contact details

Build time: 3 weeks. Total investment: within our Starter tier.

The First 30 Days

In the first 30 days after launch, something unexpected happened: the sales team started getting calls from people who had already spent 25–35 minutes in the configurator. They knew the product. They knew the price range. They just had a few specific questions before committing.

"I had a call last week that was 12 minutes long and ended with a $14,000 deposit," their sales director told us. "That never happened before."

60-Day Results

  • Close rate: 18% → 62%
  • Average call length: 40 minutes → 14 minutes
  • Time to quote: 3–5 days → same session (for ballpark) + 24 hours (for formal quote)
  • Lead volume: Up 3.4x (more people completing the funnel to submission)
  • Average ticket size: Up 18% (customers self-selecting add-ons)

What We Learned

This project reinforced something we'd seen in previous builds: the configurator doesn't just improve conversion — it changes the nature of the sales relationship. When a buyer has already spent 30 minutes designing their project, they arrive at the sales call as a partner, not a prospect.

The rep's job becomes logistics, not persuasion. And that's a much easier job to do well.
